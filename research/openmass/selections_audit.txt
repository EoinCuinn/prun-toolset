CONCLUSION
################################################################################

1. Was the collinearity real or an artefact? LARGELY AN ARTEFACT of the old feature matrix.
   Building features from billOfMaterial.quantities[] injected the game-derived hull-plate
   and SSC counts, which scale almost perfectly with totalVolume (corr 0.996 / 1.000) — the
   model was effectively predicting volume from volume. Rebuilding from user-controlled
   selections[] removes those circular columns. The rank deficiency that remains (41 of 55
   with intercept) is the benign dummy-variable trap (each one-of slot sums to the intercept),
   which is structural and does NOT harm held-out prediction.

2. Does the selections-only model predict totalVolume? YES, for the well-sampled design
   space: 76 of 87 within 2%, and 0.27% mean on the 59 common-option builds. The formal
   10-fold CV still FAILS the 2%/5% bar (mean 5.40%, max 126%) — but that failure is driven
   ENTIRELY by a handful of builds whose single distinguishing part is captured <=3 times.

3. Do we need more captures? YES — but far fewer and more targeted than the previous
   capture_checklist.txt implied. That checklist was built to break "slot-sum collinearity"
   (pure-STL ships, tank/bridge/reactor swaps). Those relations are now shown to be the
   dummy-trap ARTEFACT, not a data deficiency, so SECTION B of that checklist is largely
   UNNECESSARY. What is actually required is simply MORE SAMPLES of the under-sampled OPTIONS
   listed above — aim for >=4-5 blueprints each, at varied sizes:
     - HULL_PLATES_ADVANCED, HULL_PLATES_HARDENED (2 each today)  [old checklist Section C]
     - CARGO_BAY_TINY / VERY_SMALL / MEDIUM / HIGH_VOLUME (1-2 each)
     - COMMAND_BRIDGE_SHORT (2), HIGH_G_SEATS_ADVANCED (3), REPAIR_DRONES_LARGE (3)
     - STL_FUEL_TANK_LARGE (3), FTL_REACTOR_HIGH_POWER (2)
     - the CLNY exotics VORTEX_FUEL_TANK / VORTEX_REACTOR / HABITATION_MODULE (1 each)
   Then re-run THIS selections-only CV. It should pass once every option used by a build has
   >=4-5 independent examples. No pure-STL / slot-swap "collinearity-breaking" builds needed.

CAVEAT unchanged: totalVolume is game-computed; this shows a linear model on user selections
predicts it well where the options are sampled. The one common-option outlier (BP-VUNR-1627,
15.7%) hints at a small residual non-linearity worth watching, but it is not systemic.
