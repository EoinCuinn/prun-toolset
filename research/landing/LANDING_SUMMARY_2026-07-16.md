## Purpose and headline

This document consolidates the landing-distance investigation into a single standalone record. It is written to be precise about what is measured versus assumed, and fair to the prior work it rests on. Where a number is quoted it is taken verbatim from the source file named alongside it — the flight-planner source `prun_flight_planner.html`, the verification scripts and their outputs in `prun-flight-capture/landing-rng/`, and the capture corpus itself. The whole result is an independent confirmation and extension of your own PRNG law; almost nothing here is new physics, but the seed provenance is now nailed down from our side.

Three headline conclusions, stated up front and defended below.

First, the take-off/landing **landing distance** is not a physics quantity at all — it is a single deterministic pseudo-random draw. Your law reproduces it exactly on our captures: `d = k(planet)·(13 + 4·r)`, with `r = new java.util.Random(UUID.hashCode(missionId)).nextDouble()`. On our data the per-planet constant `k = d/(13+4r)` is constant across differing missionIds to a within-planet coefficient of variation of **0.0000%** over 18 planets, and a parameter-free cross-check (observed `d₁/d₂` versus the PRNG-predicted factor ratio) matches to **0.0000%** over 27 planet/mission pairs. The band `d ∈ [13k, 17k)` gives expected value `15k` and spread `±2/15` — i.e. ±13.3%, exactly, not an empirical "±15%".

Second, the seed is **client-supplied**. The `missionId` UUID is generated by the game client and sent in the outgoing `SHIP_FLIGHT_CALCULATE_TEST_FLIGHT` request; the server echoes it back verbatim and derives the landing from it — confirmed on **860/860** request↔response joins. Real committed flights (`SHIP_FLIGHT_FLIGHT`) drop the `missionId` from their frame but inherit the `missionId` of the plan calc that spawned them: recovering it by joining on `(planet, exact stlDistance)` and re-deriving `r` predicts the real landing at **0.00000%** error on **21/21** real flights. There is no separate real-flight mechanism.

Third — and this is the correction to the obvious next thought — the seed is **not steerable in normal play**. A disassembly of the game bundle shows the `missionId` is minted with `crypto.getRandomValues()`, an OS CSPRNG. Because the seed derives from a client-chosen UUID and `r = f(missionId)` computes entirely offline, the draw is selectable *in principle*; but the stock client draws that UUID from cryptographic entropy, so a player cannot make it land on a favourable value. The honest operational model is **read-it-don't-seed-it**: once the client mints and sends the `missionId`, `r` is computable from the wire before the server replies, but absent that, ±13.3% is the irreducible uncertainty. The fixed-tile hypothesis for real flights is tested but not closed — evidence leans against it.

## 1. What was tested, in chronological order

### 1.1 Distance-model rewrite (2026-07-14, prior)

The immediately prior state, for context: the per-engine landing tables had already been discarded in favour of a universal landing-*distance* model driven by the destination planet's radius and pressure, with an observed ±15% scatter treated as an "approach-orbit re-roll". The planner's mean estimator, in `prun_flight_planner.html`, is the frame-distance formula `d_LDG = 926 × (R_m/1e6)^0.96 × (1+P)^(-0.42)` (line 822), i.e. the expected value `15k`. This estimator is Taiyi Bureau's distance work and is unchanged by anything below; the present workstream explains the ±15% band around it, not the band's centre.

### 1.2 Corpus catalogue (2026-07-15)

Every local capture file was catalogued in one pass — **80 unique `.jsonl` files, 43,149 message frames, 14 message types** (`landing-rng/catalogue.js`, `catalogue2.js`, `catalogue_out.json`). This surfaced the two frame types the crack depends on: `ACTION_COMPLETED ▸ SHIP_FLIGHT_MISSION` (test-flight results, carrying the `missionId` and per-segment `stlDistance`) and `SHIP_FLIGHT_FLIGHT` (real committed flights). The same pass confirmed `BLUEPRINT_BLUEPRINTS.performance` exposes `totalVolume` and `operatingEmptyMass` directly for the 44 distinct blueprints in `LNG_TEST_TIME_3/blueprint_data.jsonl` — relevant to the empty-mass workstream, not to landing.

### 1.3 PRNG law confirmation on landing segments (2026-07-15)

Your law was ported to Node and run against every LANDING segment in the test-flight captures (`landing-rng/rng_test.js`). Java's `UUID.hashCode` (the 128→32-bit fold) and `java.util.Random.nextDouble()` were reimplemented with a 48-bit BigInt LCG, `r` computed per `missionId`, and `k = d/(13+4r)` fitted per planet. Two checks: within-planet constancy of `k` across different missionIds, and a parameter-free ratio check that needs no `k` at all.

### 1.4 Client-seed and real-flight verification (2026-07-15)

Two joins over the corpus (`landing-rng/seed_origin.js`, `seed_crack.js`, `seed_verify.js`). First, join outgoing `SHIP_FLIGHT_CALCULATE_TEST_FLIGHT` requests to their `ACTION_COMPLETED` responses by `actionId` and compare the request's `missionId` to the one the server used. Second, join real `SHIP_FLIGHT_FLIGHT` landings to test-mission twins by `(planet, exact stlDistance)` and re-derive `r` from the twin's `missionId`.

### 1.5 Bundle investigation (2026-07-16)

The binary question left open by the above — is the client `missionId` seedable/predictable, or true entropy? — was answered by disassembling the pulled game bundle (`bundle.1ac60af678af4c00.js`). Read-only static analysis: locate the UUID generator and the dispatch site, and determine which RNG feeds it.

## 2. What was found, with numbers

### 2.1 The PRNG law reproduces landing distance exactly

Landing distance obeys `d = k(planet)·(13 + 4·r)`, `r = new java.util.Random(UUID.hashCode(missionId)).nextDouble()`, `seed = UUID.hashCode(missionId)`, taking the first `nextDouble()` draw of a freshly-seeded Java stock LCG. `r ∈ [0,1)` gives `d ∈ [13k, 17k)`, so `dmin = 13k`, `dmax = 17k`, expected value `15k`, and half-width `2k/15k = 2/15 = ±13.3%` — the "±15%" seen in earlier observation is this exact `±2/15`, slightly overstated.

On our captures (`rng_test.js`):

- `k = d/(13+4r)` is constant per planet across differing missionIds: **18 planets, worst within-planet coefficient of variation 0.0000%**.
- Parameter-free: for planet/mission pairs, the observed distance ratio `d₁/d₂` equals the PRNG-predicted factor ratio `(13+4r₁)/(13+4r₂)` with no `k` involved: **27 pairs, maximum relative error 0.0000%**.

This is your law (per-planet `k`, Java-`Random` seed hypothesis), validated end-to-end on an independent capture set. Your own validation stands at **R² = 1.000000 across 4,482 planets and ~86k flights**; ours is the same law reproduced to machine precision on the subset we hold, plus the seed-provenance work below.

### 2.2 The seed is client-supplied and server-echoed — 860/860

Joining outgoing calc requests to their responses by `actionId` (`seed_origin.js`): in **860 of 860** joined pairs the `missionId` the client sent in `SHIP_FLIGHT_CALCULATE_TEST_FLIGHT` is byte-identical to the `missionId` the server returned and computed the landing from. The client generates the UUID; the server trusts it. This is the fact that makes the whole draw predictable from the client side — the entropy that determines the landing enters the system on the wire, in a field we can read.

### 2.3 Real flights use the same seed — 21/21 at 0.00000%

Real committed flights (`SHIP_FLIGHT_FLIGHT`) do not carry the `missionId` in their own frame; they carry a flight id and `shipId`, and the destination bottoms out at `SYSTEM/PLANET` with no finer tile identifier. But joining each real landing to a test-mission twin by `(planet, exact stlDistance)` and re-deriving `r` from the twin's `missionId` predicts the real landing distance at **0.00000% error on 21/21** real flights (`seed_crack.js`, `seed_verify.js`). So a real flight inherits the `missionId` of the plan calc that spawned it, and runs the identical PRNG draw as a preview — there is no separate real-flight mechanism.

An earlier dead end worth recording because it is what pointed at the answer: the real flight id was tried as the seed under three hashings (128-bit fold à la `UUID.hashCode`, and Java `String.hashCode` on dashed and undashed forms) and reproduced `r` on **0/13** flights (`seed_hunt.js`). The flight id is not the seed; the plan's `missionId` is.

### 2.4 The bundle: the missionId is CSPRNG, unsteerable at source

Static disassembly of `bundle.1ac60af678af4c00.js`. The dispatch site (offset 4,797,518) passes the `missionId` in as an argument; the generator `Wo()` (offset 1,978,919) is a textbook UUID v4 — 16 bytes, version and variant bits set, RNG fed by `crypto.getRandomValues()` (OS CSPRNG entropy pool). The bundled `uuid` module exposes an `e.random`/`e.rng` override seam, but it is never exercised — every call site is `Wo()` with no args. `Math.random` appears 60 times in the bundle and **zero** times in the `uuid` module. No code path seeds the UUID.

The consequence is the third headline. The draw is *deterministic given the missionId* and the missionId is *client-supplied*, so it is selectable in principle — but the stock client mints it from cryptographic entropy, so it is **unsteerable at source**: a player cannot cause a favourable value to be drawn. Steering would require forging the outgoing `missionId` before it is sent (which the 860/860 echo shows the server would honour) — that is active frame injection, not normal play, and this document documents the mechanism, not a method. For the read-only planner the model is: expected value `15k` when the `missionId` is not seen, exact when it is read from a live capture.

### 2.5 Fixed-tile hypothesis: tested, inconclusive, leaning dead

The open question inherited from your work was whether real flights *to an established base* land on a fixed tile rather than re-rolling. Our data leans against it but does not close it. Point in favour of "no fixed tile": the 21/21 real-flight matches in §2.3 show those landings used the missionId draw, not a constant per-destination value. Points that keep it open: the 8 real executed flights we hold all fall inside the `[13k, 17k]` band and are scattered across it, consistent with the same PRNG as previews; the only two repeat destination-pairs (VH-331g, AW-006b) landed at different distances, but were flown by *different ships*, so a base/tile confound is not excluded. The decisive test is the same ship landing at the same base two or more times — absent from current captures. It would be enabled by capturing the outgoing `SHIP_FLIGHT_CREATE_FLIGHT` frame, which carries the definitive `missionId → flightId` link for real flights; that capture is parked, since it is not needed for the planner's build-comparison goal.

## 3. The fuel model this sits alongside (unchanged context)

Landing *fuel* — as distinct from distance — is the same STL burn physics as take-off, and is settled: `fuel = k × flow × √(dist / accel_eff)`, `accel_eff = min(actual_accel, maxGFactor × 9.80665)`, with your closed-form constant `k = (4/0.06) × √(350.11/13.71) = 336.9` (planner comment, line 1755; empirical fit `≈338`, 0.3% off). Per-engine flow rates in the planner: glass 0.015, fuel-saving 0.0075, standard 0.015, advanced 0.02, hyperthrust 0.03 (lines 301–305). The g-factor cap uses `G0 = 9.80665` (line 363). Fuel unit masses `STL_UNIT_MASS = 0.06`, `FTL_UNIT_MASS = 0.05` (lines 366–367). None of this is affected by the landing-distance PRNG; it is included only so the two landing quantities are not conflated.

## 5. What remains open, and the experiment that would close it

**The fixed-tile hypothesis is the one genuinely open item.** Evidence leans dead — 21/21 real flights used the missionId draw — but the confound (repeat destination-pairs flown by different ships) prevents a clean refutation, and a base-specific override for owned bases cannot be excluded from current data. The experiment that closes it is small and well-defined: the **same ship landing at the same base two or more times**, captured with the outgoing `SHIP_FLIGHT_CREATE_FLIGHT` frame so the real flight's `missionId` is recorded directly rather than inferred by distance-join. If the two landings share a distance that does *not* match the missionId draw, there is a fixed tile; if they scatter within the band and match their respective draws, the hypothesis is dead. Landing distance is deterministic to machine precision, so this is not noise-limited — it needs the specific repeat captured.

**The selectability implication is documented, not open.** The mechanism is fully characterised: client-chosen UUID, server-echoed (860/860), `r` computable offline, but minted from CSPRNG so unsteerable without forging the outgoing frame. There is nothing left to measure there; it is recorded as a property of the mechanic, and the planner stays read-only.

The `missionId` provenance and the CSPRNG finding rest on a single bundle version (`bundle.1ac60af678af4c00.js`); a future bundle could in principle change the generator, so the "unsteerable" conclusion is version-scoped and worth re-checking if the client updates materially.
