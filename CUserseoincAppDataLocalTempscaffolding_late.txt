18.7 What the framework fixes, and the open problem
We close by stating, as precisely as the foregoing allows, what the velocity-inheritance picture
determines about the reduction and what it does not. The distinction is the central honesty of the
paper: the orbital mechanics above is exact and complete, but it supplies the kinematic ingredients
of the reduction, not the reduction itself. The map that converts the inherited velocity into a
charged distance is internal to the server and is not derived here.
The framework determines three things rigorously.
The sign, that is, which direction is reduced. By (258) the endpoint flight-path angle reverses
sign with the travel sense, so the projection of the inherited circular velocity onto the chord is
positive on exactly one of the two directions. The reduced branch is the direction on which that
projection is positive, and the full-arc branch is its reverse. This is a sign, fixed by the kinematics,
and it matches the empirical split into a reduced and an unreduced branch.
The direction-dependence. Because the two endpoints carry opposite-sign flight-path angles (258)
and opposite-sense magnitude mismatches (274), the reduction is not symmetric under O ↔ P; the
framework predicts the asymmetry between the two travel directions, again without a coefficient.

23 Scope and the open problem
The exact arc is closed in special functions, ℓ = 2aE(ψ,e2), and the reduction is characterised:
an effective distance, not a path (Proposition 11.1); not a real-modulus elliptic arc on a third of
the branch (Proposition 13.2); not a separable product (Proposition 14.1); of functional rank two
(Proposition 15.1); with a non-elementary asymptotic leading coefficient (Proposition 16.1). These
results bound the reduction out of every elementary family the geometry suggests and give it a
precise description as a rank-two deformation of the arc.
What remains open is the generating map. Section 12 fixes the kinematic origin, the orbital
velocity the ship inherits, and the limits it must respect, but not the coefficient or the higher-order
angular shape, which are produced by the server’s internal reduction of that velocity to a charged
distance. A first-principles derivation of that map, returning the leading coefficient c (ψ) and the
2
rank-two coupling of Section 15, is the single step that would carry the leading approximation to
the exact law. The eliminations above show it will not be an elementary function of (e,ψ).

24 Structural placement
It is worth recording where the transit law sits among the standard structures of the arc problem.
The length of an ellipse is the canonical second-kind integral, and the transit distance is exactly
that length over the symmetric sub-arc. Two distinctions organise the analysis.
The first is between the two kinds of integral. The first-kind integral F and its inverse, the
Jacobi amplitude, parametrise position along the curve and are inverted by the functions sn,cn,dn,
elementary in the parameter u; the second-kind integral E measures arc length and is not so inverted.
The transit distance is a second-kind quantity, which is why no parametrisation by elementary
functions of a single variable reproduces it; the arc accumulates at the rate dn2 along the natural
parameter u (Section 7), and its integral, the second-kind value E(ψ,m), is not an elementary
function of sinψ.
The second is between the perimeter and its elementary surrogates. The complete value 2aE(e2),
the half-perimeter, has no elementary closed form, and the elementary approximations to it are
surrogates, accurate near e = 0 and degrading toward e = 1 exactly as the leading reduction law of
Section 16 degrades. The reduction studied here is a deformation of the same non-elementary object,
and the eliminations of Part III show the deformation is itself non-elementary, a single rank-two
correction beyond the leading separable surrogate.
Finally, arcs on the ellipse do not add linearly: the second-kind integral has no simple addition
law, in contrast to the first-kind integral and the Jacobi functions of Section 7. The directional
reductionisadifferentkindofdefect, avelocity-inducedshorteningratherthanafailureofadditivity,
but both vanish in the circular limit. The transit law is thus a self-contained instance of second-kind
arc geometry: an exact arc, plus a non-elementary, rank-two, velocity-induced deformation on one
travel direction.
25 Conclusion
The in-system transit distance rests on the Hohmann transfer ellipse. The Hohmann semi-major
axis forces the endpoint symmetry cosE +cosE = 0, which collapses the arc to the single elliptic
O P
integral ℓ = 2aE(ψ,e2), exact and the whole distance on half the flights. On the other half the ship
inherits the origin body’s orbital velocity and the charged distance becomes an effective distance
below every path. That reduction is non-elementary by four independent routes and is precisely
a rank-two deformation of the arc, with the exact value carried as a tabulated surface and the
rank-one term as the closed-form approximation. The combined law matches the server to a median
0.030%, and the open problem is the kinematic map that generates the reduction.
