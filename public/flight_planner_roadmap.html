✓ Resolved since last revision: the "540 km/s defies physics" puzzle
This morning's finding that HRT's orbit implied ~107× a sun-like star's mass was a units error, not a real anomaly. The game's orbital clock runs 20× faster than real elapsed seconds (confirmed in project_flight_dynamics.md: worldTime = ref + (unixT−ref)×20). Correcting for that brings the implied GM within 2.6% of VH-331's actual star-mass-derived GM. HRT orbits an ordinary, slightly-sub-solar-mass star — nothing exotic.
✓ Resolved July 8, 2026: Radiation damage — real, confirmed, implementation parked
July 7 conclusion ("no radiation term") was premature and is reversed. Full AU sweep across NL-534 (O-class) on two independent ships, plus a pre-registered a→h prediction test that breaks the AU/distance confound, establishes: radiation damage is real, implemented, and scales as k·AU⁻² per Mkm — k ≈ 0.045 %·AU²/Mkm, roughly ship-independent, within 1.7× of Aem's independent coefficient. The a→h residual (+4.68% ship 1, +4.25% ship 2) was predicted before flight and confirmed on both ships. Whipple shielding reduces the meteoroid floor but leaves radiation unchanged — the two channels are independent. The anti-rad plate does nothing — non-functional, not merely net negative. Aem's shielded column in his KQ-451 sheet was back-computed from the tooltip (cell S48 = 0.305×0.0734), not measured — there was no shield effect to measure. His unshielded AU⁻² curve is independently reproduced. APPROACH and LANDING remain confirmed dead channels. Implementation parked: path integration along the transfer ellipse is non-trivial, star-class dependence (B/A/F/G) uncalibrated, and radiation only bites on long inner-system STL legs near O/B stars — rare in normal play. See parked section below and handover doc dated July 8.
